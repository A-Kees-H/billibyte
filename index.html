Proposal to illion-ise the -Byte System

Ask the average person how many bytes are in a Gigabyte, and they'll undo your tie the strength of their blank stare. Ask an engineer and there's a good chance they'll have to at least think about it. Everyone knows how big an [American] billion is, and most people know how big a trillion is too (the size of the old British billion). So here's my proposal for making the system more intuitive by aligning the -byte prefixes with the corresponding powers of 1000: Easier, no?:

New Term Equivalent Old Term
1 byte 1 byte 1 byte
1 kilobyte 1,000 bytes 1 kilobyte
1 millibyte 1,000,000 bytes 1 megabyte
1 billibyte 1,000,000,000 bytes 1 gigabyte
1 trillibyte 1,000,000,000,000 bytes 1 terabyte
1 quadrillibyte 1,000,000,000,000,000 bytes 1 petabyte
1 quintillibyte 1,000,000,000,000,000,000 bytes 1 exabyte

... and so on.

Note on British and American billions: Presumably due to the rise of the term and existence of "billionaires" and the lack of common use cases of 10^12 to reassert itself with, the British definition of "billion" has died a rapid death. While reviewing this article, a friend noted his displeasure with my implicit acquiescence to this Yank newspeak, and I sympathised with the displeasure, if not the practicality, so I felt it only fair to include a reminder that a British billion is by rights a million million (10^12), and the American billion (10^9), would rightfully be called a milliard; however, 10^9 billion, and even 10^12 trillion are now concretely stuck in even the British vernacular. Evading this simple truth would defeat the object of the proposal
